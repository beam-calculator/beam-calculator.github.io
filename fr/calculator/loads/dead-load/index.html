Assumptions & Limits

  • Entered layer values remain project hypotheses et do not replace a verified quantity takeoff.
  • The page prepares charge entrees but does not apply full design combinations or code partial factors automatically.
  • Changing tributary width, local peaks, or an uncertain charge path require a more detailed model.

Notes techniques
  • This page is strongest at the charge-takeoff stage, before the member response check begins.
  • Treat partition et service allowances as project hypotheses. They can dominate the dead charge on lighter floor systems.
  • If the supported area changes along the poutre, use more than one tributary width or split the member into charge zones instead of forcing one averaged value.
Calculation Method
  • Area dead charge: g_k = g_finishes + g_topping + g_slab + g_ceiling + g_services + g_partitions. The calculatrice sums the entered permanent layers into one characteristic area charge.
  • Line charge: w = g_k x b_tr. Area charge is converted into line charge through tributary width.
  • Member charge: W = w L. The total unfactored member dead charge is useful pour early appui et framing studies.
Questions frequentes
  • Should live charge be included here? No. This page is pour permanent actions only. Add imposed charge separately so dead et live components remain transparent before combinations are applied.
  • Can I use the slab field pour acier deck or bois deck self weight? Yes. That field is the main structural self-weight slot. It can represent whichever primary deck or slab layer carries the build-up.
  • Why is there a simple-appui reaction resultat on a dead-charge page? Because early framing work often needs a quick appui-charge estimate even before the full poutre model is opened.
