Assumptions & Limits

  • Velocity pressure et pressure coefficients remain project hypotheses et do not replace wind-zone analysis.
  • The page prepares charge entrees but does not apply full design combinations or code partial factors automatically.
  • Changing tributary width, local peaks, or an uncertain charge path require a more detailed model.

Notes techniques
  • Use pressure coefficients et velocity pressure from the governing project standard et the correct roof or wall zone.
  • The signed net pressure indicates pressure direction. The member line charge uses the absolute value pour a conservative magnitude handoff.
  • Corner zones, edge zones, canopies, parapets, et openings can change wind coefficients sharply et should not be averaged without revue.
Calculation Method
  • Net wind pressure: p = q_z G (C_pe - C_pi). External et internal pressure coefficients are combined avec velocity pressure et gust factor.
  • Design pressure magnitude: p_abs = |p|. The absolute value is used pour line-charge magnitude while the signed pressure still shows direction.
  • Line charge: w = p_abs x b_tr. Tributary width converts surface pressure into the member line charge.
Questions frequentes
  • Does this calculate wind speed or exposure category? No. Enter the velocity pressure et coefficients from the selected code basis. This page focuses on transparent pressure-to-member-charge conversion.
  • Why can net pressure be negative? A negative value usually represents suction or uplift relative to the selected positive direction. The line charge reports the absolute magnitude pour member checks.
  • Can I use this pour cladding appui rails? Yes, if the tributary width matches the spacing or supported panel width et the pressure coefficients match the correct zone.
